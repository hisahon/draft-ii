Developments

Methods and tools developed within IONO-DIET for near-real-time (NRT) analysis of ionospheric disturbances.

MethodNRT Clustering

From: Maletckii, B., Astafyeva, E. (2024). Near-Real-Time Identification of the Source of Ionospheric Disturbances. Journal of Geophysical Research: Space Physics, 129(11), Open Access

Abstract: The ionosphere is characterized by a large number of disturbances generated in response to a wide range of phenomena, including natural hazards, space weather and man-made events. Identification of the origin of ionospheric disturbances (ID), especially in real or near-real-time (NRT), is an extremely difficult task. This work presents an approach for automatic and NRT-compatible detection and recognition of the source of ionospheric disturbances in TEC time series measured by GNSS. The main idea is to characterize IDs from known sources (spatio-temporal features and frequency content) and, in NRT, compare new IDs to these reference characteristics.

Supporting Figure. Clustering of ionospheric disturbances driven by six sources based on spatio-temporal features (instantaneous velocities, dominant frequencies, and distances between ionospheric centres).

MethodD1-GNSS-RT

From: Maletckii, B., Astafyeva, E. (2021). Determining spatio-temporal characteristics of coseismic travelling ionospheric disturbances (CTID) in near real-time. Scientific Reports, 11(1), 1–16. Open Access

Abstract: This work presents a method to automatically detect co-seismic travelling ionospheric disturbances (CTID) and determine their spatio-temporal characteristics (velocity and azimuth) in near-real time (less than ~15 minutes after an earthquake). The method provides instantaneous velocities that allow tracking CTID evolution and estimating the CTID source location in NRT, and introduces real-time travel-time diagrams to validate correlation with the source and estimate propagation speed.

Supporting Figure. (a) Explanation of the D1 technique using a GNSS triangle (stations A, B, C) to derive velocity and azimuth. (b) Ionospheric localization of CTIDs from velocity vectors.

MethodNRT–TTD Fitting

From: Maletckii, B., Astafyeva, E. (2022). Near-Real-Time Analysis of the Ionospheric Response to the 15 January 2022 Hunga Tonga–Hunga Ha'apai Volcanic Eruption. Journal of Geophysical Research: Space Physics, 127(10). Open Access

Abstract: This work introduces NRT methods to determine instantaneous velocities using an interferometric approach and dTEC/dt, and proposes an automatic approach to estimate apparent propagation velocity from near-real-time travel-time diagrams (TTDs). The methods support low spatial resolution networks and 30-s cadence data, enabling NRT spatio-temporal analysis of TEC responses to smaller-amplitude events.

Supporting Figure. Application of the NRT–TTD method (near-field, 30-s data) and fitted velocity estimates from TTD slope.

AlgorithmAIDE (Machine-learning-based NRT CID Detection)

From: Brissaud, Q., Astafyeva, E. (2022). Near-real-time detection of co-seismic ionospheric disturbances using machine learning. Geophysical Journal International, 230(3), 2117–2130. Open Access

Abstract: This framework enables near-real-time detection of co-seismic ionospheric disturbances (CIDs) using machine learning: classification of waveforms (CID vs noise), picking of arrival times, and association across a satellite network in NRT. The approach supports rapid imaging of surface displacements from ionospheric observations.

Supporting Figure. Example outputs for the 2014 Iquique earthquake: slip projection, CID detections, and association classes.

ModelAnalytical AGW Simulation & Rapid CSTD Energetics

From: Sanchez, S. A., Kherani, E. A. (2024). A New Analytical Simulation Code of Acoustic-Gravity Waves of Seismic Origin and Rapid Co-Seismic Thermospheric Disturbance Energetics. Atmosphere, 15(5), 592. Open Access

Abstract: The study presents an analytical simulation code for seismic-origin acoustic-gravity waves (AGWs), resolving governing equations in the time–altitude and wavenumber domain. It supports rapid tracking of AGW arrivals in the thermosphere and fast formation of co-seismic thermospheric disturbances (CSTDs), enabling scenarios favorable for early forecasting.

Supporting Figure. Example ray-tracing results showing travel times using an average sound speed estimate.
